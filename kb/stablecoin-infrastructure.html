Summary

FXC Intelligence published the first independent buyer's guide benchmarking stablecoin payments infrastructure vendors. The key insight: stablecoin infra is not a single "alternative rail." It's a layered stack of interoperating systems that creates significant complexity for cross-border payments providers.

Two provider models are emerging:

  1. Modular point solutions that address specific layers of the stack
  2. Vertically integrated, end-to-end infrastructure providers

Implications for TreasuryPath

1. Multi-Rail Complexity Is Exploding

Corporate treasury teams are going from managing 2-3 rails (bank wires, ACH, SWIFT) to potentially 5-8+ rails including stablecoins. Each rail has its own settlement times, compliance requirements, liquidity pools, and custody overhead.

This is exactly what TreasuryPath's Cash Control Tower solves. As rails multiply, the need for an orchestration layer with unified visibility, intelligent routing, and automated reconciliation becomes critical.

2. The "Stablecoin Sandwich" Pattern

Fiat in → stablecoin transfer → fiat out. For this to work at scale, treasury teams need real-time visibility into stablecoin positions alongside fiat balances, automated FX conversion, cross-chain/off-chain reconciliation, and audit trails that span both worlds.

No legacy TMS handles this. Kyriba doesn't. SAP Treasury doesn't. This is greenfield.

3. B2B Is the Beachhead

B2B payments are 60% of stablecoin volume and growing 733% YoY. These are exactly TreasuryPath's target customers.

  • Supplier payments across borders → Payment routing + FX optimization
  • Intercompany transfers → Cash pooling + liquidity management
  • Yield on idle stablecoin balances → Sweep optimization
  • Multi-entity cash visibility → Cash Control Tower

4. We're the Orchestrator, Not the Infrastructure

TreasuryPath doesn't need to be a stablecoin infrastructure provider. We need to be the rail-agnostic treasury intelligence layer that sits above all of them. Whether payments flow over SWIFT, ACH, stablecoins, or any future rail, TreasuryPath provides the unified control plane.

5. The Compliance Moat

As banks issue their own stablecoins, the compliance landscape fragments. Each stablecoin has different regulatory jurisdictions, reporting requirements, and audit standards. A treasury platform that bakes compliance verification into every transaction across every rail becomes essential.
